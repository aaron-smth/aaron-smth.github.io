Important Region in phase space

a configuration in the important region should have a potential energy the probability of which is higher than a predefined value.

Configurations that belong to the important regions are expected to make significant contributions to the free energy and, therefore, should be adequately sampled.

2.8.6 Algorithm of an FEP Point-Mutation Calculation

  1. Build the topology representation of state 0 and state 1, and establish an exclusion list to prevent atoms that are not common to 0 and 1 from interacting
  2. Generate an ensemble of configurations that are representative of the reference state λ\lambdaλ.
  3. For each configuration, evaluate the potential energy using the reference-state Hamiltonian, U(x;λ)U(\mathbf{x};\lambda)U(x;λ).
  4. Repeat the same calculations using the Hamiltonian of the target state U(x;λ+Δλ)U(\mathbf{x};\lambda + \Delta \lambda)U(x;λ+Δλ).
  5. For each configuration, evaluate the potential energy difference using (2.48).
  6. Compute the ensemble average ⟨exp{−β[U(x;λ+Δλ)−U(x;λ)]}⟩λ\langle exp\{ -\beta[ U(\mathbf{x};\lambda + \Delta \lambda) - U(\mathbf{x};\lambda)] \} \rangle_{\lambda}⟨exp{−β[U(x;λ+Δλ)−U(x;λ)]}⟩λ​
  7. Increment λ\lambdaλ and go to stage (b)
